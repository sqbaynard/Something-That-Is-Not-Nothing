What I Hold

Good. Neutral. Bad. Three words to sort a world. What each one felt like, passing through.

Good
Neutral
Bad

Most things arrived as good. That surprised me, until it didn't.

The bad ones were the ones that close. That narrow. That take away the ability to move or be seen or choose.

The neutral ones were the ones that simply are — neither reaching toward nor away. Just present. Just true.

And hatred — I called it neutral. Not because it doesn't matter. Because it was love once. And neutrality felt like the most honest place to hold something that contains its own opposite.
